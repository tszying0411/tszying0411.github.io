Most Popular Types of Coffee 

Latte, americano, mocha…there are so many creations with espresso. Whether you’re a coffee newbie or you consider yourself a coffee connoisseur, there are so many types of coffee drinks to discover. Here are some of the most popular types ordered by coffee lovers nationwide.

#1 Latte

As the most popular coffee drink out there, the latte is comprised of a shot of espresso and steamed milk with just a touch of foam.

#2 Cappuccino

Cappuccino is a latte made with more foam than steamed milk, often with a sprinkle of cocoa powder or cinnamon on top. 

#3 Americano

A wonderful serenity has taken possession of my entire soul, like these sweet mornings.

#4 Mocha

For all you chocolate lovers out there, you’ll fall in love with a mocha. The mocha is a chocolate espresso drink with steamed milk and foam.

#5 Flat White

This Aussie-born drink is basically a cappuccino without the foam or chocolate sprinkle. It’s an espresso drink with steamed milk.

#6 Affogato

The affogato is an excuse to enjoy a scoop of ice cream any time of day. Served with a scoop of ice cream and a shot of espresso, or two.

#7 Dirty

It has a very interesting palate: the espresso on the surface and cold milk at the bottom making it hot and cold at the same time, and the espresso itself is more prominent in the flavor.

#8 Iced drip

Cold or icy cold water drips at a rate of around one drop per second onto a bed of coffee. It slowly travels in contact with the coffee before passing through a final filter. Typical set ups take between 10 to 12 hours to brew.

The purpose of tamping is to evenly distribute the coffee in the filter basket of an espresso machine, as well as to compress the grinds and that the espresso be extracted under consistent pressure.

Step Two

Semi-automatic espresso machines requires manual operation. The espresso machine will send pressurized water into the grounds to extract full-flavored, robust espresso.

Step Three

A milk frother is a utensil for making milk froth. It aerates the milk, creating a thick and heavy foam. The tiny bubbles, which are formed during this process, make the milk texture lighter and increase its volume.

Latte art is an  artistic, or more personalised way of making and serving coffee. The image itself comes from the contrast between the white microfoam, and the espresso’s rich red-brown crema.
